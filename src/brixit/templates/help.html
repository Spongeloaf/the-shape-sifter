How to use BriXit

Basics

The image at the top of the page is a picture of a lego part taken from my conveyor belt. Below the image is a search box. Enter the name of the part in the box, and a list of search results from bricklink will be displayed. Scroll down through the results to find the correct entry and select it. Scroll back up and click the submit button. The part will be tagged with the bricklink number, and the next image is displayed.

There is a link next to each search result, that says "BrickLink". This will open a new tab that shows the catalog entry of the part on BrickLink .com

There are four buttons to handle cases where the image is poor or the part cannot be identified

  • Conveyor Belt Pictures of the conveyor belt. Clean pictures with no visible parts or debris.
  • Bad Image The picture is cropped poorly, contains multiple parts, is too dark, or the part is not clearly visible.
  • Skip Part If the picture is good, but you cannot find a part by searching, skip this part and move on.
  • Unknown Wheel If the picture is good, but it contains a wheel that you cannot identify.

If you're struggling to identify wheels/tires, this reference from Sariel may be helpful.

Dimensions

Part dimensions are order-agnostic, and you can use "*", "x", or "by" as separators, with or without spaces. The following search terms are equivalent:

  • "2 x 4"
  • "2 by 4"
  • "4 x2"
  • "2*4"
  • "4* 2"

Multi-dimensional parts

Some parts have 3 dimensions in their name, such as Slope, Curved 3 x 2 x 2/3 with Two Studs, Wing End you might expect that you could find such a part with a search query such as "curved slope 3x2", however this will not work. Due to time restrictions and the complexity of the search engine, searching with two dimensions i.e. "3x1" will not return results like "3x1x1". I will attempt to fix this in the future, however it is not a simple problem.

Difficult parts

During testing, we have identified a some parts that proved to be particularly difficult to identify, mostly due to having unexpected or overly complex names. I have been adding them to this page in the hope that it will save others some headache
